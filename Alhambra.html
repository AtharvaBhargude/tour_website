Alhambra

The Alhambra, located in Granada, Spain, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most iconic landmarks of Moorish architecture. Originally built in 889 AD and later transformed into a royal palace in the 13th century, it is celebrated for its intricate Islamic art, stunning gardens, and breathtaking views of the Sierra Nevada mountains.

Key Features

Built: Originally in 889 AD, expanded in the 13th century
Architectural Style: Islamic architecture with intricate tilework and carvings
Highlights: Nasrid Palaces, Generalife Gardens, and the Alcazaba fortress
Scenery: Panoramic views of Granada and the Sierra Nevada

Visiting Information

Times: Open daily, hours may vary by season
Entry Fee: €14 per adult, discounts available for children, seniors, and students
Tours: Guided tours and audio guides available for an immersive experience
Special Offers: Combined tickets with other historic landmarks in Granada

Dining Options

Parador de Granada: Fine dining in a historic setting
Alhambra Café: Offers refreshments and light snacks
Nearby Restaurants: Explore Andalusian cuisine at local eateries
