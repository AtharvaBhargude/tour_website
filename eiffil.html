Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world, located in Paris, France. It was originally constructed for the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World's Fair) to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution1. Designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel, the tower stands at 324 meters (1,063 feet) tall and was the tallest man-made structure in the world until the completion of the Chrysler Building in New York in 1930.

Key features

Height: 324 meters (1,063 feet)
Construction: Completed in 1889
Designer: Gustave Eiffel
Materials: Over 18,000 individual iron parts, held together by 2.5 million rivets

Visiting Information

Times: 9:30 AM - 11:00 PM (April to September), 9:30 AM - 10:00 PM (October to March)
Prices: €11.8 to €29.4 for adults, €3 to €14.7 for children and young people
Tours: Available in French and English, lasting around 90 minutes, with a maximum group size of 12 people
Special Offers: 10% discount on certain products in the stores and certain restaurants for guided tour participants

Dining Options

Madame Brasserie: Located on the 1st floor, offering a view of the Paris skyline
Buffet on the Esplanade: Available for guided tour participants with a 10% discount
