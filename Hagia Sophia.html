Hagia Sophia

Hagia Sophia, located in Istanbul, Turkey, is a renowned architectural marvel with a rich history spanning over 1,500 years. Originally built as a cathedral in 537 AD, it later became a mosque and is now a museum. Famous for its massive dome, intricate mosaics, and historical significance, it remains one of the most visited landmarks in the world.

Key Features

Built: 537 AD by Emperor Justinian I
Architectural Style: Byzantine with Islamic elements
Highlights: The grand dome, mosaics, and minarets
Significance: A symbol of the intersection of cultures and religions

Visiting Information

Times: Open daily, hours may vary depending on events
