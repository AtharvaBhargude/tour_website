Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty, located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, USA, is a globally recognized symbol of freedom and democracy. Designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and dedicated in 1886, it was a gift from France to commemorate the centennial of American independence. The statue stands 93 meters (305 feet) tall from base to torch.

Key Features

Height: 93 meters (305 feet) from base to torch
Construction: Completed in 1886
Designer: Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi
Material: Copper sheets supported by a steel framework
Iconic Torch: Represents enlightenment and freedom

Visiting Information

Times: 9:00 AM - 4:30 PM (ferry schedules may vary)
Prices: $24.50 for adults, $12 for children (includes ferry and pedestal access)
Tours: Guided tours available with an emphasis on historical significance
Special Offers: Combination tickets with access to Ellis Island

Dining Options

Liberty Café: Located on Liberty Island, offering snacks and light meals
Ellis Island Café: Offers hearty meals and a range of beverages
