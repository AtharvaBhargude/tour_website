Palace of Versailles

The Palace of Versailles is one of the most magnificent landmarks in the world, located in Versailles, France. Originally a hunting lodge for Louis XIII, it was transformed and expanded by his son, Louis XIV, into a grand palace and the center of political power in France. The palace is renowned for its opulent architecture, exquisite gardens, and rich history.

Key Features

Location: Versailles, France
Construction: Expanded between 1661-1715
Architects: Louis Le Vau, Jules Hardouin-Mansart
Gardens: Designed by André Le Nôtre, covering approximately 800 hectares

Visiting Information

Times: 9:00 AM - 6:30 PM (April to October), 9:00 AM - 5:30 PM (November to March)
Prices: €18 for Palace ticket, €20 for Passport ticket (access to all areas), free for children under 18
Tours: Guided tours available in multiple languages, including English and French
Special Offers: Discounted rates for groups and special events

Dining Options

Angelina Tea Room: Located inside the palace, famous for its hot chocolate and pastries
La Petite Venise: Located in the gardens, offering Italian cuisine
