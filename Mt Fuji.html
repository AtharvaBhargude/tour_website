Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji, or "Fujisan," is Japan's highest mountain and a symbol of the nation. Located on Honshu Island, it stands at 3,776 meters (12,389 feet). This iconic stratovolcano has cultural, historical, and spiritual significance, inspiring artists and pilgrims for centuries.

Key Features

Height: 3,776 meters (12,389 feet)
Formation: Last eruption in 1707-1708
Significance: UNESCO World Heritage Site (2013)
Features: Perfectly symmetrical cone shape

Visiting Information

Climbing Season: July to early September
Access: Tours and public transport from Tokyo and surrounding areas
Activities: Hiking, photography, and cultural exploration
Nearby Attractions: Fuji Five Lakes, Hakone hot springs
