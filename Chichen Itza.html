Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an iconic archaeological site located in the Yucatán Peninsula, Mexico. It was built by the Maya civilization and was one of their major centers from around 600 to 1200 AD. The site is famous for its incredible architecture, particularly the Temple of Kukulcán, also known as El Castillo, which reflects advanced knowledge of astronomy and mathematics.

Key Features

Height: El Castillo stands at 30 meters (98 feet)
Construction: Completed around the 9th–12th centuries AD
Designer: Maya civilization
Materials: Limestone blocks intricately carved and placed

Visiting Information

Times: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM daily
Prices: $25 to $50 USD for adults, free for children under 13
Tours: Available in Spanish and English, lasting around 2 hours
Special Offers: Combined tickets with Cenote Ik Kil for discounts

Dining Options

On-Site Restaurants: Authentic Mayan cuisine near the entrance
Nearby Food Stalls: Fresh local snacks like tacos and tamales
