Louvre Museum

The Louvre Museum is one of the largest and most visited art museums in the world, located in Paris, France. Originally a royal palace, it was transformed into a museum during the French Revolution in 1793. It houses over 38,000 artifacts, including famous works like the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo.

Key Features

Established: 1793
Collection: Over 38,000 works of art
Famous Artifacts: Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo
Location: Palais Royal, Paris

Visiting Information

Hours: 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM (Closed on Tuesdays)
Prices: €17 for regular entry, free for visitors under 18 and EU residents under 26
Tours: Guided tours in multiple languages, lasting approximately 2 hours
Special Offers: Free admission on the first Sunday of every month (October to March)

Dining Options

Le Café Mollien: Offers classic French pastries and coffee with a view of the pyramid
Louvre Food Court: A variety of international cuisines for a quick meal
