Grand Canyon

The Grand Canyon, located in Arizona, USA, is one of the world's natural wonders. Carved by the Colorado River over millions of years, this breathtaking landmark stretches 446 kilometers (277 miles) long, up to 29 kilometers (18 miles) wide, and more than a mile deep. It is renowned for its awe-inspiring vistas, layered rock formations, and diverse ecosystems.

Key Features

Length: 446 kilometers (277 miles)
Depth: Over 1,800 meters (6,000 feet)
Formation: Erosion by the Colorado River over millions of years
Geological Significance: Layers of rock dating back nearly 2 billion years

Visiting Information

Times: Open 24 hours a day, year-round
Entry Fee: $35 per vehicle, $20 per person on foot or bicycle
Tours: Available, including helicopter tours, rafting trips, and guided hikes
Special Offers: Annual passes available for frequent visitors
