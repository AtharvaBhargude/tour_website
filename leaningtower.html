Leaning Tower of Pisa

The Leaning Tower of Pisa, located in Pisa, Italy, is a world-famous freestanding bell tower known for its unintended tilt. Constructed between 1173 and 1372, this iconic structure was part of a cathedral complex. Its tilt began during construction due to an unstable foundation, making it a symbol of architectural resilience and ingenuity.

Key Features

Height: Approximately 56 meters (183 feet) on its tallest side
Construction: Completed in 1372
Architect: Bonanno Pisano (attributed)
Materials: Marble and limestone
Special Tilt: Around 4 degrees, stabilized to prevent further leaning

Visiting Information

Times: 9:00 AM - 8:00 PM (seasonal variations may apply)
Prices: €18 for tower access; additional fees for guided tours
Tours: Guided tours available in multiple languages, lasting about 30 minutes
Special Offers: Combination tickets for access to the cathedral and nearby monuments

Dining Options

Local Cafés: Numerous cafés and restaurants nearby offering authentic Italian cuisine
Specialty Foods: Famous for gelato, pizza, and pasta dishes unique to the region
