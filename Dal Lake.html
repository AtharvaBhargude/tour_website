Dal Lake

Dal Lake, often referred to as the "Jewel in the crown of Kashmir," is a stunning freshwater lake located in Srinagar, Jammu and Kashmir, India. This iconic lake is famous for its floating gardens, vibrant Shikaras (traditional wooden boats), and luxurious houseboats. It also holds immense cultural and historical significance, attracting visitors from around the world.

Key Features

Area: Approximately 18 square kilometers (6.9 square miles)
Highlights: Floating gardens, Mughal gardens along the shores, and Shikara rides
Unique Aspect: Seasonal change in the lake's beauty, with lotus blooms in the summer
Local Culture: Integral to the livelihoods of the local community

Visiting Information

Times: Accessible all day, but sunrise and sunset are the most picturesque times to visit
Activities: Shikara rides, houseboat stays, photography, and bird-watching
Entry: Free for visitors, but Shikara and houseboat services are chargeable
Best Time: May to November for pleasant weather
