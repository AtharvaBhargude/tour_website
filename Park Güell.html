Park Güell

Park Güell, located in Barcelona, Spain, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a masterpiece by architect Antoni Gaudí. Originally envisioned as a residential park, it now serves as a public space showcasing whimsical designs, colorful mosaics, and stunning views of the city.

Key Features

Architect: Antoni Gaudí
Year Completed: 1914
Design: Combines natural elements with intricate mosaics and unique structures
Highlights: The Dragon Staircase, Hypostyle Room, and the Serpentine Bench

Visiting Information

Times: Open daily, hours vary seasonally
Entry Fee: €10 per adult, discounts available for children, seniors, and students
Tours: Guided tours available to explore Gaudí’s architectural brilliance
Special Offers: Skip-the-line tickets for hassle-free entry

Dining Options

Park Güell Café: Offers light snacks and refreshments
Nearby Restaurants: Explore Catalan cuisine at local eateries around the park
