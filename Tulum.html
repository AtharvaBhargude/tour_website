Tulum

Tulum, located on the Caribbean coastline of Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula, is one of the most stunning archaeological sites of the ancient Mayan civilization. Famous for its cliffside location overlooking turquoise waters, Tulum served as an important trading hub for the Maya, connecting land and sea routes.

Key Features

Location: Positioned on cliffs 12 meters (39 feet) above the sea
History: Dates back to 1200–1450 AD
Main Attraction: El Castillo (The Castle), a prominent structure used as a lighthouse and ceremonial site
Materials: Constructed using limestone and stucco

Visiting Information

Times: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM daily
Prices: $10 USD for adults, free for children under 5
Tours: Guided tours available in Spanish, English, and other languages
Special Offers: Discounts on eco-tourism packages combining Tulum with nearby cenotes

Dining Options

Beachfront Cafés: Fresh seafood and local delicacies served with ocean views
Eco-Lodges: Sustainable dining experiences with a focus on farm-to-table ingredients
