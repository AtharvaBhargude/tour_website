Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is an exquisite white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653, the Taj Mahal is an enduring symbol of love and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is renowned for its symmetrical design and intricate inlay work that incorporates semi-precious stones.

Key Features

Height: 73 meters (240 feet)
Construction: Completed in 1653
Designer: Ustad Ahmad Lahori
Materials: White marble sourced from Makrana, Rajasthan, and inlaid with semi-precious stones

Visiting Information

Times: Open from sunrise to sunset, except Fridays
Prices: ₹50 for Indian citizens, ₹1100 for foreign tourists, free for children below 15 years
Tours: Guided tours available in multiple languages
Special Offers: Combined tickets with Agra Fort for discounts

Dining Options

Nearby Restaurants: Fine dining options offering Mughlai and Indian cuisine
Local Food Stalls: Savory street food like chaats and samosas
