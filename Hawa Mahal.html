Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is an iconic monument located in Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, this stunning five-story structure is renowned for its intricate latticework and 953 small windows, called jharokhas, which were designed to allow royal ladies to observe daily life and festivals without being seen.

Key Features

Height: 15 meters (50 feet)
Construction: Built in 1799
Architectural Style: Fusion of Mughal and Rajput architecture
Materials: Pink and red sandstone

Visiting Information

Times: Open from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM daily
Prices: ₹50 for Indian citizens, ₹200 for foreign tourists, free for children under 5
Tours: Guided tours available in multiple languages
Special Offers: Discounts on combined tickets with City Palace and Jantar Mantar

Dining Options

Nearby Cafés: Traditional Rajasthani snacks and chai with views of Hawa Mahal
Local Restaurants: Authentic Rajasthani thalis and Mughlai cuisine
