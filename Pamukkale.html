Pamukkale

Pamukkale, located in southwestern Turkey, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its white travertine terraces and thermal springs. Known as the "Cotton Castle," it is a natural wonder that has attracted visitors for centuries with its breathtaking beauty and ancient ruins of the nearby Hierapolis city.

Key Features

Geography: White limestone terraces formed by calcium-rich waters
Highlights: Thermal pools, Hierapolis ruins, and the Cleopatra Pool
History: Ancient spa destination dating back to the Roman Empire
Unique Experience: Walking barefoot on the travertine terraces

Visiting Information

Times: Open daily, hours vary by season
Entry Fee: 700 Turkish Lira per person, including access to Hierapolis
Tours: Guided tours available for historical and natural exploration
Special Offers: Combined tickets for Hierapolis and Cleopatra Pool
