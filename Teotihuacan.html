Teotihuacan

Teotihuacan, known as the "City of the Gods," is an ancient Mesoamerican city located in the Basin of Mexico. Famous for its massive pyramids and complex urban planning, Teotihuacan was a significant cultural, political, and economic center from around 100 BCE to 750 CE. The Pyramid of the Sun and the Pyramid of the Moon are its most iconic structures, drawing visitors from all over the world.

Key Features

Main Structures: Pyramid of the Sun, Pyramid of the Moon, and the Temple of the Feathered Serpent
Area: Spans approximately 20 square kilometers (8 square miles)
Construction: Developed between 100 BCE and 250 CE
Materials: Stone, clay, and volcanic rock

Visiting Information

Times: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM daily
Prices: $5 USD for adults, free for children under 13
Tours: Guided tours available in multiple languages
Special Offers: Discounts for group bookings and early morning tours

Dining Options

Nearby Restaurants: Authentic Mexican cuisine at eateries close to the site
