Colosseum

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an ancient Roman structure located in the heart of Rome, Italy. Built between AD 70–80 under the emperors Vespasian and Titus, it is the largest amphitheater ever constructed, capable of holding up to 50,000 spectators. It hosted gladiatorial contests, animal hunts, and public spectacles.

Key Features

Height: 48 meters (157 feet)
Construction: Completed in AD 80
Capacity: Approximately 50,000 spectators
Materials: Concrete and travertine

Visiting Information

Hours: 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM (Last entry one hour before closing)
Prices: €16 for adults, €2 for EU citizens aged 18-25, free for children under 18
Tours: Guided tours available in multiple languages, lasting around 1 hour
Special Offers: Combined tickets include access to Palatine Hill and the Roman Forum

Dining Options

Café Colosseo: Located nearby, offering coffee and light snacks
